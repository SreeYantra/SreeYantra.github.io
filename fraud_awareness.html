What is cybercrime

Cybercrime refers to illegal activities that are committed using the internet or other digital technologies, like computers, smartphones, or networks. It's like breaking the law, but instead of doing it in person, the crime happens online or through digital means. Overview Cybercrime is on the rise, even the best antivirus software tools can't stop scammers who target the uneducated people. Some of the most damaging and costly scams involve what's known as "social engineering." That's when fraudsters use time-tested techniques of deception and emotional manipulation, tricking people into divulging personal information, financial information and allowing the attackers to remote control their devices knowingly or unknowningly. People of all ages and socioeconomic positions are potential targets mainly uneducated people. They're more likely to have amassed savings, and they're perceived to be more isolated or perhaps less computer savvy. "What is changing is the criminals' ability to connect with us because of the rise in usage of smartphones.
